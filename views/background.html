I Ching

How readings are derived

When consulting the I Ching, one begins with sources of entropy… traditionally either bundles of yarrow-stalks or tossed coins. From these, an algorithm is followed to arrive at a “reading”, which is then open to contemplation and interpretation. In the most popular form of the practice, this reading amounts to 6 bits of information, traditionally rendered as “hexagram” of six open/yin or closed/yang lines, e.g.: ䷺

There is also the notion of extra energy or state in this system… less frequently, any line can be “moving” or unstable, in transition from open to closed, or vice versa. In this way, any reading has varying degrees of transition of instability, for example in this reading, we see that the two center lines are moving from yin to yang: ䷺ → ䷫

In addition to being a rich, multi-dimensional divination system worthy of more study, this is also an impressive amount of entropy for an algorithm that’s nearly 2.5 million . There are many spaces here for something… nature? Randomness? Some collective consciousness? To assert itself over the human world of ordered, stable binaries. Notes:

  • The move from divination from stalks to coins changes the likely reading outcomes.
  • Universe impacts reading -> reading adjusts to describe universe

Influence on modern computation

In refining on the binary system, Gottfried_Wilhelm_Leibniz was directly inspired by the I-Ching, titling his paper "" Explanation of the binary arithmetic, which uses only the characters 1 and 0, with some remarks on its usefulness, and on the light it throws on the ancient Chinese figures of Fu Xi".

password generation

To this end, we propose a strong password generator rooted in the I Ching tradition. In this way, we can harness one particularly important modern need for entropy, namely cybersecurity, as an invitation to bring meditation on the underlying nature of reality into the quotidian and often frustrating world of password prompts and reset emails.

One goal would be to harness the original, often forgotten original yarrow-stick algorithm for consulting the Oracle, which contains more entropy than the coin-tossing method, and make this complex analog algorithm more accessible. Watching the algorithm work becomes a moment for connection with the 3000 years of commentary and exploration of the I Ching system, the passage of time, and the nature of randomness / randomness of nature. The output of this algorithm would be a detailed, nuanced I Ching reading which can offer both insight into the questions one is currently facing in their lives, as well as a strong, memorable password. To this end, we hope to use the binary nature of the I Ching to offer a variety of “down-conversions” of your hexagram to password strings compatible with a variety of “strong password” requirements held by different services.
